Bar Celoneta Sangria Bar

  • Friend

Open Today

View Hours
Contact 931722280

Calle Sevilla 70, Barcelona, Spain, 08003

Bar and restaurant by the beach offering organic vegan food with tapas, burgers, salads, raw food and vegan cheese. Every Thursday is paella day. Specializes in sangria (with and without alcohol) and has organic wine and beer. The raw desserts are sugar- and gluten-free. Mon-Thu 6:00pm-11:30pm, Fri-Sat 1:00pm-11:30pm, Sun 1:00pm-8:00pm

Category: Vegan, Italian, Organic, Mediterranean, Beer/Wine, Spanish

Pet Sounds by the Beach Boys - Edit

Posted by hack_man on 13 Apr 2016.

We eventually got to Bar Celoneta in December 2015 after years of just thinking about going. This is a beautiful vegan sangria and tapas bar located in the real life world of Barcelonetta amongst a residential setting. The range of beers here is exceptional. I went for an Amstel Oro Tostada. A gorgeous and cheeky dark Dutch number. We also had a sangria that blew away anything that I've ever tasted before. It was more like a cocktail made with class and passion. They are individually made and not mass produced like at most sangria places. To accompany this we had a patata bravas com curry. This was genuinely scintillating. The interior is colourful and welcoming. It's very friendly, and run by a passionate vegan who was a genuine pleasure to speak with. You could spend hours here over a few beers and tapas dishes whilst surfing on the WiFi. On a second visit in April 2016, we chose a selection of tapas dishes including tempeh & aubergine skewers, traditional Catalan tomato bread, "Vegan Aliens' (layers of vegan cheese and roasted courgette) and patatas bravas. Spicy spicy, not for kids!! It's not Michelin starred but I would give it an Estrella.

Pros: fantastic beer and sangria, excellent tapas, very friendly

Wonderful time at this cozy vegan/vegetarian oasis - Edit

Posted by Dfeinphilly on 16 Sep 2014.

My wife and I had the pleasure of enjoying a great meal here after a Sunday evening walk on the Barceloneta beach. The restaurant is located on a small side street a few blocks from the beach & it's easy to find due to a sign they had on the sidewalk at the corner at the end of the block.

The menu is very vegan friendly. The vegan fare is tapas focused but they also offer homemade veggie burgers that would probably be a meal on their own.

Since my wife and I are vegan, we didn't really get to immerse ourselves in Barcelona's extensive tapas scene but the offerings at Bar Celoneta brought us into the tapas realm.

We thoroughly enjoyed patatas with a 'Montserrat' inspired sauce, tasty mixed greens salad with walnuts, delicious grilled veggies that were full of flavor (herbs and spices) and were topped with with a great romesco sauce, circles of perfectly cooked and spiced tempeh with lime shavings and lastly, delicious, expertly cooked smoked tofu that really took on an inviting smokey flavor.

I had my first Estrella Galicia of the trip (I'm a craft beer nerd so I tried Spanish craft beers the entire trip) and my wife had a lovely Cava/Campagna/Red Vermouth drink.

The ambience of this establishment is great - cozy, comfy, there are books designed to look like flying birds hanging from the ceiling, the lighting might be a little too dark but once again, it adds to the cozy vibe.

The service was perfect. Roberto, the owner, served us promptly, checked in on us throughout the meal and made us feel like friends. We enjoyed a great conversation with him at the end of our meal and we will certainly come back here when we return to Barcelona.

Pros: great vegan food, prompt, attentive service, comfy and cozy

Cons: could use a little more lighting, no air conditioning (still not too hot)

A veggie place on the beach - Edit

Posted by JonJon on 12 Aug 2014.

We went there during our day at the beach one saturday noon. The place is really convenient for such a day if you're vegan/vegetarian since there is not a lot of veggie places around. As they propose a brunch on week-end, we do not had the menu card that day (the one outside looked good!) but we were offer only their three brunch options. We took the basic one that consisted of one juice, one main dish (there was two choices of vegan omelette), the buffet ad libitum and tea/coffee for 13,50 euros which was really fair. The staff was friendly but seems sometimes a little lost (we sometimes waited a moment).

The vegan omelette were really good and well presented, the buffet was nice but lacks a little bit vegan options in comparison to vegetarian ones. Moreover, they should be more clearly labelled (some were but some were not). I took a seitan dish, some fried vegetables, hummus and bread. On the whole, it was a good experience but could have been even better!

Pros: good food, brunch on week-end, vegan options, affordable, beach location

Cons: brunch could offer more vegan options, staff sometimes seems lost
